1. Introduction to Indus (Harappan) Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization (IVC), also known as the Harappan Civilization, was one of the world's earliest urban civilizations. It flourished approximately between 2600 BCE and 1900 BCE in the northwestern region of the Indian subcontinent. The civilization developed mainly along the Indus River and its tributaries. The Harappans achieved remarkable progress in urban planning, engineering, chemistry, metallurgy, medicine, trade and craftsmanship.

Why is Harappan Civilization Important in Chemistry?
  • Knowledge of metals and alloys.
  • Manufacturing of ceramics and pottery.
  • Use of natural pigments.
  • Development of dyeing techniques.
  • Production of perfumes and cosmetics.
  • Glass and bead manufacturing.
  • Medical and pharmaceutical practices.

Timeline of Harappan Civilization

  • 7000 BCE: Early settlements at Mehrgarh.
  • 3300 BCE: Early Harappan Phase.
  • 2600 BCE: Mature Harappan Phase.
  • 1900 BCE: Decline begins.
  • 1300 BCE: Late Harappan Phase.

Scientific Achievements

  • Grid-pattern city planning.
  • Underground drainage systems.

  • Chemical knowledge in pottery and dyes.
  • Mass production techniques.
Exam Point: Harappan Civilization is considered one of the earliest examples of scientific urban planning and technological innovation in human history.

2. Later Pottery

Pottery refers to objects made from clay and hardened by firing. Harappan pottery is famous for its uniformity, strength, artistic beauty and mass-production methods. The discovery of large quantities of pottery indicates the existence of skilled potters and organized industries.

Raw Materials Used

  • Clay
  • Water
  • Natural pigments
  • Mineral additives

Manufacturing Process

  1. Collection of clay.
  2. Removal of impurities.
  3. Mixing with water.
  4. Shaping on potter wheel.
  5. Drying under sunlight.
  6. Painting and decoration.
  7. Firing in kiln.

Chemistry Behind Pottery

Clay contains hydrated alumino-silicates. When heated:

  • Water evaporates.
  • Organic matter burns away.
  • Silicates fuse together.
  • Pottery becomes hard and durable.

Types of Pottery

Type Description
Red Ware Most common pottery
Black Painted Ware Decorative pottery
Storage Jars Food and grain storage
Cooking Pots Used for preparing food

Importance

  • Evidence of chemical knowledge.
  • Shows understanding of heat treatment.
  • Indicates industrial production.
  • Demonstrates artistic development.

3. Knowledge of Metallurgy During and After Harappan Period

Metallurgy is the science and technology of extracting, purifying and utilizing metals. The Harappans were among the earliest metallurgists in human civilization. Archaeological evidence shows extensive use of copper, bronze, gold, silver, lead and tin.

What is an Ore?

An ore is a naturally occurring mineral from which a metal can be extracted economically. Examples:

  • Copper Ore
  • Tin Ore
  • Lead Ore
  • Iron Ore

Metals Known to Harappans

Metal Uses
Copper Tools and weapons
Bronze Statues and tools
Gold Jewelry
Silver Ornaments
Lead Weights
Tin Bronze production

Copper Extraction

Copper oxide ores were heated with charcoal. The carbon present in charcoal reduced copper oxide to metallic copper.

Reaction: 


Copper Oxide + Carbon
      ↓
Copper + Carbon Dioxide

Bronze Technology

Bronze is an alloy formed by mixing copper and tin. Bronze is stronger, harder and more corrosion resistant than pure copper.

Property Copper Bronze
Hardness Medium High
Strength Moderate High
Durability Good Excellent

Applications of Metallurgy

  • Agricultural tools
  • Knives
  • Axes
  • Spears
  • Ornaments
  • Containers
  • Trade products
Important Examination Point: Harappans were among the first civilizations to use alloy technology effectively. The development of bronze significantly improved the quality of tools and weapons.
