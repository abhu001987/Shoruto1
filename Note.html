UNIT 1 : Indigenous Technology in Harappan Period

Introduction to Indus (Harappan) Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization (Harappan Civilization) flourished around 2600–1900 BCE. It was one of the world's earliest urban civilizations. Major cities included Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro, Dholavira and Lothal. The civilization was known for advanced town planning, drainage systems, trade, metallurgy and craftsmanship.

Later Pottery

  • Made using potter's wheel.
  • Red pottery with black painted designs.
  • Used for storage, cooking and rituals.
  • Shows artistic and technological development.

Knowledge of Metallurgy During and After Harappan Period

Metallurgy is the science of extraction and utilization of metals. Harappans were familiar with copper, bronze, gold, silver, tin and lead.

Applications:
  • Tools and weapons
  • Ornaments
  • Household objects
  • Trade goods

Weights and Measurements

  • Standardized stone weights.
  • Decimal system of measurement.
  • Used in trade and taxation.
  • Ensured accuracy and fairness.

Medical Science

  • Knowledge of medicinal plants.
  • Evidence of dental treatment.
  • Treatment of wounds and fractures.
  • Use of herbal medicines.

Jewellery Making

  • Made from gold, silver, copper, shells and gemstones.
  • Included necklaces, earrings, bangles and beads.
  • Advanced bead drilling and polishing techniques.

Dyeing Technology

  • Use of natural dyes.
  • Indigo for blue colour.
  • Turmeric for yellow colour.
  • Madder root for red colour.

Pigments

Pigments are colouring substances obtained from minerals, plants and charcoal.

  • Red Ochre
  • Yellow Ochre
  • Black Carbon Pigment

Philosopher's Stone

Legendary substance believed by ancient alchemists to convert base metals into gold and provide immortality. Although mythical, it inspired many chemical experiments.

Wootz Steel

  • High-quality steel produced in ancient India.
  • High carbon content.
  • Strong and durable.
  • Used in making swords and tools.
  • Famous worldwide.

Gold

  • Yellow, lustrous metal.
  • Malleable and ductile.
  • Used in ornaments and trade.

Silver

  • Shiny white metal.
  • Excellent conductor of heat and electricity.
  • Used in utensils and ornaments.

Mercury

  • Only metal liquid at room temperature.
  • Symbol: Hg
  • Used in scientific instruments and medicine.
  • Toxic metal.

Tin

  • Soft, silver-white metal.
  • Used in making bronze.
  • Corrosion resistant.

Lead

  • Heavy, soft metal.
  • Used in pipes, weights and construction.
  • Poisonous if accumulated in the body.

Gunpowder

Composition:

  • 75% Potassium Nitrate
  • 15% Charcoal
  • 10% Sulphur

Uses:

  • Fireworks
  • Mining
  • Weapons

Glass Making

Raw Materials:

  • Silica
  • Soda Ash
  • Limestone

Process:

  1. Mixing
  2. Heating
  3. Moulding
  4. Cooling

Paints

Paints contain pigments, binders and solvents. Used for decorating walls, pottery and artistic works.

Perfumes

  • Prepared from flowers, herbs and essential oils.
  • Used for personal care and religious purposes.
Keywords: Indus Civilization, Harappan Civilization, Pottery, Wootz Steel, Glass Making, Paints, Perfumes.

Analgesics

Drugs that relieve pain without causing loss of consciousness.

  • Aspirin
  • Paracetamol
  • Ibuprofen

Antibiotics

Chemical substances that kill or inhibit bacteria.

  • Penicillin
  • Streptomycin
  • Amoxicillin
  • Tetracycline

Antidepressants

Medicines used to treat depression and anxiety disorders.

  • Fluoxetine
  • Sertraline
  • Amitriptyline

Antihypertensive Drugs

Medicines used to lower blood pressure.

  • Amlodipine
  • Atenolol
  • Losartan

Antipyretics

Medicines that reduce fever.

  • Paracetamol
  • Aspirin
  • Ibuprofen

Anticoagulants

Medicines that prevent blood clotting.

  • Heparin
  • Warfarin

Bronchodilators

Medicines that widen air passages in the lungs.

  • Salbutamol
  • Theophylline

Vaccines

Biological preparations that provide immunity against diseases.

  • BCG Vaccine
  • Polio Vaccine
  • Hepatitis-B Vaccine
  • COVID-19 Vaccine

Antacids

Substances that neutralize excess stomach acid.

  • Magnesium Hydroxide
  • Calcium Carbonate
  • Sodium Bicarbonate

Diuretics

Medicines that increase urine production.

  • Furosemide
  • Hydrochlorothiazide
  • Spironolactone

Drug Metabolism (ADME)

A - Absorption

Entry of drug into bloodstream.

D - Distribution

Movement of drug throughout the body.

M - Metabolism

Chemical breakdown of drugs mainly in liver.

E - Excretion

Removal of drugs from body through urine, bile and sweat.

Keywords: Analgesics, Antibiotics, Antidepressants, Antihypertensives, Antipyretics, Anticoagulants.

UNIT 3 : Pollution and its Causes

Air Pollution

Definition: Contamination of air by harmful substances.

Causes:
  • Vehicle emissions
  • Industrial smoke
  • Burning fossil fuels
  • Forest fires
Effects:
  • Respiratory diseases
  • Global warming
  • Acid rain
  • Ozone depletion
Prevention:
  • Use public transport
  • Plant trees
  • Control industrial emissions
  • Use clean energy

Water Pollution

Sources:
  • Sewage
  • Industrial waste
  • Agricultural runoff
  • Oil spills
Effects:
  • Waterborne diseases
  • Death of aquatic life
  • Reduced oxygen level

Soil Pollution

Sources:
  • Pesticides
  • Industrial waste
  • Plastic waste
  • Chemical fertilizers
Effects:
  • Reduced fertility
  • Contamination of crops
  • Loss of biodiversity

Noise Pollution

Causes:
  • Traffic
  • Industrial machinery
  • Loudspeakers
  • Construction activities
Effects:
  • Stress
  • Hearing loss
  • Sleep disturbance
Prevention:
  • Use silencers
  • Control loudspeaker usage
  • Plant green belts

E-Waste Pollution

Causes:
  • Discarded electronic devices
  • Improper recycling
Effects:
  • Release of toxic metals
  • Soil contamination
  • Water contamination
Prevention:
  • Proper recycling
  • Reuse electronic products
  • Responsible disposal

Radioactive Pollution

Contamination caused by radioactive substances.

Causes:
  • Nuclear accidents
  • Nuclear weapon testing
  • Nuclear waste
Effects:
  • Cancer
  • Genetic mutations
  • Environmental damage
Prevention:
  • Safe disposal of radioactive waste
  • Strict nuclear safety measures
  • Monitoring radiation exposure

Nuclear Weapons

Weapons that derive destructive power from nuclear reactions. They release enormous amounts of energy and radiation.

Nuclear Power Plants

Facilities that generate electricity using nuclear fission reactions. Advantages include large energy production with low carbon emissions.

Chernobyl Disaster

The Chernobyl nuclear accident occurred in 1986 in Ukraine. It is considered one of the worst nuclear disasters in history. Large amounts of radioactive materials were released into the atmosphere.

Keywords: Air Pollution, Water Pollution, Soil Pollution, Noise Pollution, E-Waste Pollution, Radioactive Pollution.
